The shape of the conference

Each band flows left to right: total sessions → AI vs Not AI → theme → audience.

What stands out
  • 78.4% of the entire catalog is AI — 870 of 1109 sessions.
  • App dev is the most common theme at 302 sessions, spanning both AI and non-AI content.
  • Inside AI, App dev leads at 268 sessions — builders are clearly the primary target.
  • Outside AI, Security takes the top spot with 106 sessions.
  • 337 sessions target Developers, 315 target Leaders — the conference tilts toward the buyer side.
  • 148 sessions target infrastructure and ops engineers, 107 target data practitioners. The platform comes before the pipeline.
  • The major consulting firms together account for 36 sessions — more than any single non-Google company on the list.

Top companies speaking

Non-Google companies
  • Palo Alto Networks leads all non-Google companies with 14 sessions — a persistent presence throughout the program.
  • The major consulting firms (Accenture, McKinsey & Company, Deloitte, PwC, Cognizant, HCLTech) add up to 36 sessions combined — more than any individual non-Google company. A big slice of the conference is about enterprise adoption, with consultants as the delivery vehicle.
  • NVIDIA at #8 (9 sessions) makes the AI hardware dependency explicit — the software story at this conference runs on their chips.
  • Anthropic appears 8 times — Google's AI model investment earns a recurring stage presence.
  • Shopify (13) and Snap (5) are consumer brands, not enterprise software companies — Google is using them as scale proof points for what Cloud infrastructure can handle.
